Philosophy

Weather HQ is built for people who want real weather data without the noise. The goal is a fast, honest dashboard: no ads, no tracking, no unnecessary complexity. Data is fetched fresh on every load from open, public APIs and never routed through a backend. It runs as a PWA so it installs on your phone and works offline with the last cached fetch — the right tool for a morning glance before you head out the door.

Forecast Models

The 10-day forecast runs two independent global models in parallel: the American GFS (NOAA's Global Forecast System) and ECMWF IFS (the European Centre's Integrated Forecast System, widely regarded as the gold standard for medium-range prediction). Both are fetched fresh via Open-Meteo on every load. When the two models agree on a high or low within 3°F, the dashboard shows a single temperature — the most likely outcome. When they diverge by more than 3°F, the forecast card shows a range instead, for example 67–72°, because showing false precision would be less honest than showing the disagreement. This matters most beyond day 5, where small differences in initial conditions can compound into larger divergence. Tapping a temperature range reveals the individual model readings so you can see which model is the outlier and weigh them yourself.

Data Sources

  • Open-Meteo Forecast, current conditions, and multi-model comparison — free, open-source NWP API open-meteo.com ↗
  • NWS / NOAA Official US forecasts and active weather alerts (US locations only) api.weather.gov ↗
  • Meteostat Observed station data for recent precipitation history meteostat.net ↗
  • Open-Meteo AQI Air quality index derived from the European CAMS forecast model open-meteo.com/air-quality ↗

Architecture

Stack Vanilla JS · ES modules · no frameworks · no build step
Structure services/ fetch + normalize · ui/ render DOM · utils/ pure helpers · state/ single store
PWA Versioned service worker cache · installable on iOS & Android · offline serves last fetch
Data flow API → service normalizes → main.js orchestrates → renderer writes DOM once per refresh
